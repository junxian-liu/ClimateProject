Wildlife

Animals, from the land to the sea are effected by the climate crisis in numerous ways. From rehabilitating to adapting to a new environment, wildlife deal with the consequences of climate change more then we know. Due to this, some animals become endangered or even extinct. To learn more about the animals that have been effected by the climate crisis, click this link.

Human Involvement

Human activites are one of the main causes to climate change, specifically the extintion crisis and animals being endangered. From the excess release of fossil fuels to deforestation, humans have contributed in numerous ways towards furthering the damage of climate change

Population Size

On average, the population sizes of monitored mammals, fish, birds, reptiles, and amphibians have decreased significantly in the past few decades. As much as 68 percent since 1970 and gotten as high as 94 percent in certain parts of the worlds for groups of animals.

Extinction

With an estimation of 2 million different species of animals on Earth, and with the estimated extinction rate (10%), we observe that up to 2000 animals go extinct every year.
